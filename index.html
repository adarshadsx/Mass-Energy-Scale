= c2

What does Einstein's famous equation really mean?

(scroll down)

The "E" is for "energy". Like the number of calories in foods.

The "m" is for "mass". Related to weight.

The "c2" is the speed of light squared.

Since the speed of light is a constant of nature, you can think of this term as a conversion factor. It's how you change units of mass into units of energy, and vice versa.
